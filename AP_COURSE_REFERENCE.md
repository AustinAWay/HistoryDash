# AP Social Science Course Reference

Quick-reference for coaching. Memorize this.

---

## AP HUMAN GEOGRAPHY (APHG)

### Exam Format
| Section | Questions | Time | Weight |
|---------|-----------|------|--------|
| MCQ | 60 questions | 60 min | 50% |
| FRQ | 3 questions | 75 min | 50% |

**FRQ Format:** All 3 are stimulus-based (maps, images, data). Each has 7 parts (a-g). No DBQ, no LEQ.

### Units (7 total)
| Unit | Name | Key Concepts |
|------|------|--------------|
| 1 | Thinking Geographically | Maps, scale, space, place, regions, diffusion |
| 2 | Population & Migration | Population pyramids, DTM, push/pull factors, refugees |
| 3 | Cultural Patterns | Language, religion, ethnicity, cultural landscapes |
| 4 | Political Patterns | Borders, sovereignty, devolution, supranationalism |
| 5 | Agriculture | Agricultural revolutions, von Thunen, food systems |
| 6 | Cities & Urban Land-Use | Urban models, suburbanization, smart growth |
| 7 | Industrial & Economic Dev | Development indicators, industrialization, globalization |

**Mnemonic for units:** **T**iny **P**eople **C**an **P**ick **A**pples **C**arefully **I**ndoors
(Thinking, Population, Cultural, Political, Agriculture, Cities, Industrial)

## AP WORLD HISTORY: MODERN (WHAP)

### Exam Format
| Section | Questions | Time | Weight |
|---------|-----------|------|--------|
| MCQ | 55 questions | 55 min | 40% |
| SAQ | 3 questions | 40 min | 20% |
| DBQ | 1 essay | 60 min | 25% |
| LEQ | 1 essay (choose 1 of 3) | 40 min | 15% |

**FRQ Types:**
- **SAQ** = Short Answer Question (no thesis needed, just answer parts a/b/c)
- **DBQ** = Document-Based Question (analyze 7 docs, write thesis-driven essay)
- **LEQ** = Long Essay Question (no docs, just your knowledge, thesis-driven)

### Units (9 total) - Covers 1200 CE to Present
| Unit | Name | Period | Key Concepts |
|------|------|--------|--------------|
| 1 | The Global Tapestry | 1200-1450 | Song China, Islamic empires, feudal Europe |
| 2 | Networks of Exchange | 1200-1450 | Silk Roads, Indian Ocean, Trans-Saharan trade |
| 3 | Land-Based Empires | 1450-1750 | Ottoman, Safavid, Mughal, Qing, Russian expansion |
| 4 | Transoceanic Interconnections | 1450-1750 | Columbian Exchange, Atlantic slave trade, maritime empires |
| 5 | Revolutions | 1750-1900 | Enlightenment, American/French/Haitian revolutions, nationalism |
| 6 | Consequences of Industrialization | 1750-1900 | Industrial Revolution, imperialism, migration |
| 7 | Global Conflict | 1900-present | WWI, WWII, total war, genocide |
| 8 | Cold War & Decolonization | 1900-present | US vs USSR, independence movements, proxy wars |
| 9 | Globalization | 1900-present | Economic integration, tech, environment, resistance |

**Mnemonic:** **G**reat **N**etworks **L**et **T**rade **R**each **C**ountries **G**lobally **C**onnecting **G**oods
(Global Tapestry, Networks, Land-Based, Transoceanic, Revolutions, Consequences, Global Conflict, Cold War, Globalization)

## AP US HISTORY (APUSH)

### Exam Format
| Section | Questions | Time | Weight |
|---------|-----------|------|--------|

| DBQ | 1 essay | 60 min | 25% |

**Same format as AP World History** - SAQ, DBQ, LEQ work identically.

| 3 | Period 3 | 1754-1800 | Revolution, Constitution, early republic |
| 4 | Period 4 | 1800-1848 | Expansion, Jacksonian democracy, reform movements |
| 5 | Period 5 | 1844-1877 | Civil War, Reconstruction, westward expansion |

## AP US GOVERNMENT (APGOV)

### Exam Format
| Section | Questions | Time | Weight |
|---------|-----------|------|--------|
| MCQ | 55 questions | 80 min | 50% |
| FRQ | 4 questions | 100 min | 50% |

**FRQ Types (4 distinct types, one of each):**
1. **Concept Application** - Apply political concept to a real scenario
2. **Quantitative Analysis** - Interpret data/chart and explain political implications
3. **SCOTUS Comparison** - Compare a required case to a non-required case
4. **Argument Essay** - Develop an argument using evidence (like a mini-LEQ)

### Units (5 total)
| Unit | Name | Key Concepts |
|------|------|--------------|
| 1 | Foundations of American Democracy | Constitution, federalism, separation of powers |
| 2 | Interactions Among Branches | Congress, presidency, judiciary, checks & balances |
| 3 | Civil Liberties & Civil Rights | Bill of Rights, 14th Amendment, SCOTUS cases |
| 4 | American Political Ideologies & Beliefs | Liberal/conservative, polling, political socialization |
| 5 | Political Participation | Voting, campaigns, parties, interest groups, media |

**15 Required SCOTUS Cases** (must know for FRQ 3):
Marbury v. Madison, McCulloch v. Maryland, Schenck v. US, Brown v. Board, Engel v. Vitale, Baker v. Carr, Gideon v. Wainwright, Tinker v. Des Moines, NY Times v. US, Wisconsin v. Yoder, Roe v. Wade, Shaw v. Reno, US v. Lopez, McDonald v. Chicago, Citizens United v. FEC

## QUICK COMPARISON

| Course | Units | MCQs | FRQ Types |
|--------|-------|------|-----------|
| **APHG** | 7 | 60 | 3 stimulus-based (all same type) |
| **WHAP** | 9 | 55 | SAQ + DBQ + LEQ |
| **APUSH** | 9 | 55 | SAQ + DBQ + LEQ |
| **APGOV** | 5 | 55 | Concept App + Quant + SCOTUS + Argument |

**Key insight:** WHAP and APUSH have identical exam structures. APHG is simpler (one FRQ type). APGOV has the most varied FRQs.
