## AP Exam Recipes

### AP U.S. History (APUSH)

**Section I, Part A: Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQ)**
55 Questions | 55 Minutes | 40% of Score
Stimulus-based sets (3–4 questions each).

**Content Distribution Constraints (MCQ):**

* Unit 1 (1491–1607): 4–6%
* Unit 2 (1607–1754): 6–8%
* Unit 3 (1754–1800): 10–17%
* Unit 4 (1800–1848): 10–17%
* Unit 5 (1844–1877): 10–17%
* Unit 6 (1865–1898): 10–17%
* Unit 7 (1890–1945): 10–17%
* Unit 8 (1945–1980): 10–17%
* Unit 9 (1980–Present): 4–6%

**Assembly Rules:**

* Maintain proportional representation within ±2 questions tolerance.
* Avoid clustering more than 25% of questions in a single unit.
* Include varied stimulus types: text, images, maps.

\---

**Section I, Part B: Short-Answer Questions (SAQ)**
3 Questions | 40 Minutes | 20% of Score

* Q1 (Required): Secondary source
* Q2 (Required): Primary source
* Q3 or Q4 (Choice): No stimulus

**Content Distribution Constraints (SAQ):**

* Cover at least 2–3 different time periods.
* Ensure one question pre-1900 and one post-1900.
* Avoid all questions focusing on the same historical theme (e.g., all political).

\---

**Section II, Part A: Document-Based Question (DBQ)**
1 Question | 60 Minutes (includes 15-minute reading period) | 25%

**Content Constraints:**

* Focus on a single historical period or closely related span.
* Include 6–7 documents representing multiple perspectives.
* Avoid overuse of commonly tested periods (rotate Units 5–8).

\---

**Section II, Part B: Long Essay Question (LEQ)**
1 Question (choice of 3) | 40 Minutes | 15%

**Content Constraints:**

* Provide 3 prompts covering distinct period bands:

  * Option A: Units 1–3 (1491–1800)
  * Option B: Units 4–6 (1800–1898)
  * Option C: Units 7–9 (1890–Present)
* Each prompt must emphasize a different historical thinking skill (e.g., causation, comparison, continuity/change).

### AP World History: Modern (APWH)

**Section I, Part A: Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQ)**
55 Questions | 55 Minutes | 40% of Score
Stimulus-based sets using texts, maps, and images.

**Content Distribution Constraints:**

* Units 1–2 (1200–1450): 8–10%
* Units 3–4 (1450–1750): 20–30%
* Units 5–6 (1750–1900): 25–35%
* Units 7–9 (1900–Present): 20–30%

**Assembly Rules:**

* Ensure global balance across regions (Asia, Europe, Africa, Americas).
* No single region should exceed \~40% of questions.
* Include diverse stimulus types and perspectives.

\---

**Section I, Part B: Short-Answer Questions (SAQ)**
3 Questions | 40 Minutes | 20% of Score

* Q1 (Required): Secondary source
* Q2 (Required): Primary source
* Q3 or Q4 (Choice): No stimulus

**Content Constraints:**

* Questions must span at least 3 different time periods.
* Include at least 2 different world regions.
* Avoid thematic duplication across all three questions.

\---

**Section II, Part A: Document-Based Question (DBQ)**
1 Question | 60 Minutes | 25%

**Content Constraints:**

* Focus on a single period (typically Units 3–7).
* Include documents from at least 3 regions.
* Require cross-cultural or comparative reasoning.

\---

**Section II, Part B: Long Essay Question (LEQ)**
1 Question (choice of 3) | 40 Minutes | 15%

**Content Constraints:**

* Provide 3 prompts covering broad period bands:

  * Option A: 1200–1450
  * Option B: 1450–1900
  * Option C: 1900–Present
* Each prompt should test a different reasoning skill.

\---

### AP Human Geography (APHG)

**Section I: Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQ)**
60 Questions | 60 Minutes | 50% of Score

**Content Distribution Constraints:**

* Unit 1 (Thinking Geographically): 8–10%
* Unit 2 (Population): 12–17%
* Unit 3 (Cultural Patterns): 12–17%
* Unit 4 (Political): 12–17%
* Unit 5 (Agriculture): 12–17%
* Unit 6 (Cities): 12–17%
* Unit 7 (Development): 12–17%

**Assembly Rules:**

* At least 25% of questions must include maps or spatial data.
* Ensure balance between conceptual knowledge and applied scenarios.

\---

**Section II: Free-Response Questions (FRQ)**
3 Questions | 75 Minutes | 50% of Score

* 1 stimulus-based question (map, graph, or table)
* 2 non-stimulus questions

**Content Constraints:**

* Cover at least 3 different units.
* Include at least one applied spatial analysis task.
* Avoid repeating the same model or theory across multiple questions.

\---

### AP U.S. Government and Politics (AP US Gov)

**Section I: Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQ)**
55 Questions | 80 Minutes | 50% of Score

**Content Distribution Constraints:**

* Foundations of American Democracy: 15–20%
* Interactions Among Branches: 25–35%
* Civil Liberties \& Civil Rights: 13–18%
* Political Participation: 20–27%
* American Political Ideologies: 10–15%

**Assembly Rules:**

* Include at least 5 quantitative/data interpretation questions.
* Include Supreme Court references in \~10–15% of items.
* Ensure a mix of conceptual and scenario-based questions.

\---

**Section II: Free-Response Questions (FRQ)**
4 Questions | 100 Minutes | 50% of Score

* Q1: Concept Application
* Q2: Quantitative Analysis
* Q3: SCOTUS Comparison
* Q4: Argument Essay

**Content Constraints:**

* Each FRQ should map to a different content category where possible.
* Rotate required Supreme Court cases across exam forms.
* Argument essay must allow multiple defensible claims and evidence pathways.
